How it works

Simple for you.
Personalized for everyone.

You're one parent, not four nutritionists. We handle the complexity so you don't have to.

01
Set up every family member

Add everyone in under 2 minutes. Each person gets their own calorie and macro targets — based on their age, weight, activity level, and goal. Cutting, bulking, maintaining, peak performance. All of it, automatically calculated.

02
Log meals in 10 seconds

Snap a photo. Say what you ate. Type "chicken sandwich and fries." The AI reads it, calculates portions, and logs it. No barcode scanning. No manual entry. Log for your kids from your own phone — one account covers everyone.

03
See exactly who needs what

Every week, the app surfaces the top 3 nutritional gaps for each family member — with specific meal suggestions to fix them. No vague advice. Concrete actions for the actual people in your house.

04
One grocery list. Every gap covered.

The app generates a single consolidated grocery list that addresses every family member's nutritional needs for the week — organized by aisle. Stop buying "healthy" food that doesn't move the needle for anyone.

Common questions

We've heard the hesitations.

"I already use MyFitnessPal."

If you're tracking just yourself, MFP is great — use it. But running nutrition for a household is a different job. You need to see everyone at once, catch who's falling short week to week, and turn that into one grocery list. That's what this is built for.

"I don't have time to log every meal."

That's exactly why we built photo and voice logging. Snap your plate, say what you ate, and you're done in 10 seconds. Most families log a full day in under 3 minutes total.

"My kids won't use an app."

They don't have to. One parent account covers every family member. You log for them — in 10 seconds — from your own phone. No accounts for kids, no discipline required.

"Is this actually accurate for kids and teens?"

Yes. Targets are calculated using age- and activity-specific nutritional guidelines. Your teen athlete gets different macro targets than your 8-year-old — because they should.
